Whole Wheat Bread

A simple, no frills bread recipe adapted from years of baking simple loaves of bread.

This recipe comes from hundreds of loaves of bread I've baked. Its the most simple recipe I use and is definetly not super special, but it will get the job done, especially if you need to multiply it and make many loaves. I usually make this into a round loaf, but occasionaly use a bread pan and make a sandwich-style loaf as depicted above.

Ingredients*

  • ~ 3⅓ cups (400 grams) of King Arthur Whole Wheat Flour
  • ~ 1½ cups (350mL) of warm water
  • 1 cup (230mL) of water
  • ~ 2 Tbsp (30 mL) of honey
  • ~ 2 Tsp (11 grams) of salt
  • ~ 2¼ tsp (6.75 grams) of yeast

    • *All are approximations because I am liberal with my measurements!

Steps

  1. Activate yeast by pitching it into a bowl of warm water (1½ cups/350mL). Add in 1 Tbsp of honey and stir gently.
  2. Cover and wait about 15 minutes for the yeast to begin eating the sugars in the honey. Time varies depending on ambient temperature.
  3. Mix dry ingredients in a bowl and add the remaining 1 Tbsp of honey. After yeast rises, mix in the yeast/honey/water mixture and stir until dough ball forms.
  4. Knead until dough ball is springy but firm. Cover for at least 2 hours to let rise.
  5. Generously flour work area, then turn risen dough onto it and begin kneading for at least 10 minutes.
  6. Shape dough ball into a round loaf and place on greased baking tray or turn into a greased baking pan. Let rise for at least 45 minutes.
  7. After some time elapses, turn oven on to 425°F (220°C) and place a recessed baking tray on lowest rack.
  8. Score top of your bread. Get creative and add a pattern!
  9. Place bread onto middle oven rack, and pour remaining 1 cup of water into the tray. Close door quickly!
  10. Bake for 15 minutes, then turn oven to 375°F (190°C) and bake for 35 minutes.
  11. Remove from oven and do not cut! Wait until bread is cool.
  12. Enjoy!
